Question 2.1

1. Algorithmic vs. human bias dangers -> scaling issues, automation, no appeals, false objectivity skew, self reinforcing feedback loops. But when on human in the loop -> random sampling for human review, appeals takes processes and time, diverse oversight committee needed

2. Counteracting skewed data -> re-weight or oversample underrepresented groups, use external diverse benchmarks or scoring metrics, audit outcomes frequently. For responsibility -> it should be shared between engineers, management, and organizational culture and also  individual engineers should have the duty to raise red flags.

3. Pymetrics/HireVue advantages -> standardized assessment, reduced resume based bias, consistent evaluation, which are all good.  But for dangers -> lack of transparency, cultural bias in games, disability accessibility bias, facial recognition bias. So in overall -> potential improvement only with rigorous independent auditing and regulation
